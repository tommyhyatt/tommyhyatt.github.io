Created by Tom Hyatt and Natasha Niethamer

In 2023, The Liverpool School of Tropical Medicine celebrated its 125th anniversary by commissioning two sci-artists, Tom Hyatt and Natasha Niethamer, to create a unique piece of public engagement.

The Tropical Medicine Time Machines are three boxes, each with five 3D-printed artefacts, which trigger videos spanning the length and breadth of the institutions history and scientific achievements.

The Time Machines are hosted by AI-wen Evans: a re-animation of LSTM's first female lecturer Dr Alwen Myfanwy Evans. Travelling across space and time, each artefact features a unique interview with one of LSTM's researchers.

As well as a pop-up, interactive museum, the Time Machines are also a mini school curriculum. Accompanying workbooks (click here to download) can be adapted by teachers to suit a variety of ages.

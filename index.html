The Tropical Medicine Time Machine is a public engagement sci-artwork, designed and created by Tom Hyatt and Natasha Niethamer for the 125th anniversary of Liverpool School of Tropical Medicine (LSTM).

It was made possible with funding from the National Lottery Heritage Fund, along with contributions from PH Holt, Eleanor Rathbone Trust, Duchy of Lancaster Benevolent Fund, and The Hemby Trust.

Three unique Time Machines, each with five accompanying 3D artefacts, encompass the length and breadth of LSTM's research and legacy. Ai-wen Evans (a re-animation of LSTM's first female lecturer Dr Alwen Myfanwy Evans) takes the public on a journey through time and space, interviewing fifteen LSTM researchers along the way.
